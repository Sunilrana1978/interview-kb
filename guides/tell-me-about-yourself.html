▸ Tell Me About Yourself
~2 min · 5 paragraphs
My name is Sunil Kumar, and I bring nineteen years of IT experience across government, retail, banking, and most recently the sports technology domain at Nike. Throughout my career, I've evolved from hands-on technical roles into leadership, which has given me a unique perspective on both architecture and team dynamics. In my most recent role, I led a team of sixteen engineers and was accountable for critical system design and implementation in a high-stakes environment. I designed and implemented solutions using the AWS stack, with deep expertise in compute, networking, and storage services. This taught me how to balance architectural excellence with delivery timelines and team capability. What I'm most proud of is my ability to bridge the gap between business objectives and technical strategy. I don't just design systems — I empower teams to own them. I've successfully mentored engineers, established best practices, and ensured that our technical decisions aligned with organizational goals. My strengths are in cloud infrastructure design, distributed systems, system scaling, and making architectural decisions under constraints. On the leadership side, I'm skilled at hiring, building high-performing teams, fostering a culture of ownership, and communicating technical complexity to non-technical stakeholders. I'm now looking for a role where I can have broader impact — either as a lead cloud architect shaping the technical direction of an organization, or as an engineering manager building and scaling world-class teams. I'm excited about roles that value both technical depth and people leadership.
Script Breakdown
01 Opening — Who You Are ~20 sec

"My name is Sunil Kumar, and I bring nineteen years of IT experience across government, retail, banking, and most recently the sports technology domain at Nike."

Lead with your name, years, and domain diversity. This immediately signals seniority and breadth. Nike name-dropping is strong social proof — use it confidently.

02 Most Recent Role — Impact ~30 sec

"I led a team of sixteen engineers responsible for critical system design in a high-stakes environment. Deep expertise in AWS compute, networking and storage."

Numbers matter — "sixteen engineers" is specific and credible. Connect technical work to business stakes: high-stakes environment signals real-world pressure and trust.

03 Your Superpower — Bridge Builder ~25 sec

"I bridge the gap between business objectives and technical strategy. I don't just design systems — I empower teams to own them."

This is your key differentiator. Many engineers can code; fewer can align technical decisions with business goals AND grow people. This line separates you from IC-track candidates.

04 Your Strengths — Technical & Leadership ~25 sec

Technical: Cloud infrastructure design, distributed systems, system scaling, architectural decisions under constraints.

Leadership: Hiring, building high-performing teams, culture of ownership, communicating complexity to non-technical stakeholders.

05 Closing — What You Are Seeking ~20 sec

"I'm looking for a role with broader impact — as a lead cloud architect shaping technical direction, or as an engineering manager building and scaling world-class teams."

End with intention. This signals you know what you want, you're targeting deliberately, and you bring a growth mindset. It also naturally hands back the conversation.
